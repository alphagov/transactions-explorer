The data gives details of transactional services for every government department, agency or body: their digital take-up, costs and, for most services, the amount of transactions per year.

Data collection

We collect data from departments every three months. Data sets cover a 12 month period so that seasonal variation is balanced out. The latest data published covers January 2013 to December 2013.

You can download the full raw data set into spreadsheet-compatible CSV files.

Transactional services

Transactional services are activities like vehicle licensing, passport applications, benefit claims and filing company accounts. They involve an exchange of information, money, licences, goods or services and result in a change in the records held by government organisations.

Fuller data is given for high-volume services, which have over 750,000 transactions per year, or a departmental spend of over £750m per year. Access this data by clicking on the service names.

Some terms explained

The data is labelled with these terms:

  • digital take-up - percentage of transactions completed or partially completed through digital channels
  • cost per transaction - average cost of providing each successfully completed transaction across all channels, including web, phone, post and in person
  • total cost - overall cost per year of providing a service, including staff, IT and accommodation costs; these figures are calculated from volume and cost per transaction figures, and some rounding errors may occur
  • data coverage - proportion of requested data provided by each department

Find out how to measure cost per transaction and about other key performance indicators, including digital take-up and how to increase it.

Costs

Most services can’t be compared directly, as they include different processes. Cost per transaction isn’t a measure of cost-efficiency across different services. The notes on costs for individual services provide additional information about how cost per transaction has been calculated for each service.

Service costs

‘Taxpayers’ is used for transactional services which are funded through general taxation. Any savings made from improving the efficiency of these services will benefit the taxpayer.

‘Fees and charges’ is used for transactional services which are paid for by service users. Any savings will generally benefit users, through lower fees or charges.
